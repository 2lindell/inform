  • linguistics

A library of code to store grammatical categories of vocabulary. This is version 1.

  • Chapter 1: Setting Up

    Loading this module.

    • Linguistics Module - Setting up the use of this module.

  • Chapter 2: Excerpts

    • Excerpt Meanings - To register and deregister meanings for excerpts of text as nouns, adjectives, imperative phrases and other usages.

    • Parse Excerpts - Given an excerpt of wording, to construct S-nodes for one or more registered excerpt meanings which it matches.

  • Chapter 3: Grammatical Categories I

    • Articles and Pronouns - To define some elementary particles.

    • Cardinals and Ordinals - To parse integers seen from a grammatical point of view.

    • Determiners and Quantifiers - To create the determiners found in standard English which refer to collections of things, and to create their meanings as logical quantifiers.

    • Adjectives - To record the names of all adjectives.

    • Adjective Usages - A lightweight structure to record uses of an adjective, either positively or negatively.

    • Nouns - Nouns are an abstraction for meanings registered with the excerpt parser which serve as names for individual things or kinds of things.

  • Chapter 4: Grammatical Categories II

    • Verbs - To record the identity and different structural forms of verbs.

    • Verb Usages - To parse the many forms a verb can take.

    • Prepositions - To define prepositional forms.

    • Adverbs of Certainty - To represent levels of certainty.

    • Adverb Phrases of Occurrence - To parse representations of periods of time or of historical repetition.

  • Chapter 5: Diagramming Sentences

    • Diagrams - To construct standard verb-phrase nodes in the parse tree.

    • Verb Meanings - To abstract the meaning of a verb.

    • Noun Phrases - To construct noun-phrase subtrees for assertion sentences found in the parse tree.

    • Verb Phrases - To construct standard verb-phrase nodes in the parse tree.
